You ask, we answer.

Echo records conditions for the entire journey and stores everything on the device. At destination, anyone can scan the QR code on the label or open a link you share. No app, no login, no pairing — just tap “scan” with Bluetooth enabled. The phone reads the device automatically and sends the data straight to your Suply dashboard. No one handles or edits the data. Within seconds, the full dataset appears in the shipment timeline, aligned with milestones, delays and voyage behaviour.
Echo is not a cellular or satellite real-time tracker. Real-time devices can be useful in specific cases, but they are often expensive and frequently struggle to deliver uninterrupted data in real shipping environments. For most exporters, they are unnecessary for the majority of shipments.

Echo takes a different approach. It uses inexpensive, compact, disposable hardware together with AI that interprets vessel positions, port delays, milestones and voyage behaviour. This keeps hardware costs low while producing more meaningful context than many live devices can offer.

Traditional loggers have been common because they are cheap and simple, but modern supply chains are more complex. Their reliance on finding, recovering and manually downloading files introduces delays and ambiguity. As expectations shift toward better traceability, clearer explanations and stronger evidence for audits and claims, intelligent context built around simple hardware provides clarity that old thermographs can no longer deliver.
Echo records every two minutes and can store up to six months of data. This covers all ocean and air shipments, including delays, transshipments and extended dwell times.
Traditional loggers only provide a raw PDF or CSV with no explanation of where or when events occurred. Echo pairs temperature history with voyage behaviour, port delays and milestone intelligence, giving exporters a clear, defensible explanation instead of an isolated file. Retrieval is fully wireless and instant.
Echo uses calibrated components designed for cold-chain applications. Readings are time-stamped, stored on the device and uploaded automatically, reducing manual handling errors and producing a reliable, audit-ready dataset.
